

## references

(Be aware that references will not be printed, a link will be created by us that leads to your references in the online version of your article) 
Our reference style is embedded in Zotero and Mendeley. Please search for Wiley-WAS or download it here: https://www.zotero.org/styles?q=id%3Awiley-was 

 

Please list all references in the body text in square brackets, not superscripts. 
Provide a complete list of all references including DOI as follows:  
 

For 1-3 authors: 

Last name, Initials of the first name(s). (Year) Article name. Journal name. DOI 
Example:  
[1] Miksch L., Gutow L., Saborowski R. (2021). pH-Stat Titration: A Rapid Assay for Enzymatic Degradability of Bio-Based Polymers. Polymers. DOI: 10.3390/polym13060860.  

 

More than 3 authors: 
Last name, Initials of the first name(s). et al. (Year) Article name. Journal name. DOI 
Example:  
[2] Wei R., et al. (2012.) A high-throughput assay for enzymatic polyester hydrolysis activity by fluorimetric detection. Biotechnology Journal. DOI: 10.1002/biot.201200119. 
 
[1] Stringer C., et al. (2021). Cellpose: a generalist algorithm for cellular segmentation. Nature Methods. DOI: 10.1038/s41592-020-01018-x.

[2] Archit A., et al. (2025). Segment Anything for Microscopy. Nature Methods. DOI: 10.1038/s41592-024-02580-4.

[3] image.sc forum. (2024). Thoughts on the latest SOTA segmentation algorithms. https://forum.image.sc/t/thoughts-on-the-latest-sota-segmentation-algorithms/94106 (accessed March 2024).
